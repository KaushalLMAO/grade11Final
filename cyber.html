8.1 Digital Society and Computer Ethics

A digital society refers to a society where digital technologies are deeply integrated into everyday life. Computer ethics involves the moral principles and guidelines that govern the use of computers and technology. It addresses issues like privacy, intellectual property, and the impact of technology on society.

Key Concepts

  • Privacy: Protecting personal information in the digital space.
  • Digital Divide: The gap between those who have access to technology and those who do not.
  • Ethical Use of Technology: Ensuring technology is used responsibly and for the benefit of society.

8.2 Concept of Information Security

Information security refers to the practices and measures taken to protect data from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, or destruction. It ensures the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.

Key Principles

  • Confidentiality: Ensuring data is accessible only to authorized users.
  • Integrity: Maintaining the accuracy and consistency of data.
  • Availability: Ensuring data is accessible when needed.

8.3 Concept of Cybercrime

Cybercrime refers to criminal activities carried out using computers or the internet. It includes hacking, identity theft, phishing, and spreading malware.

Types of Cybercrime

  • Hacking: Unauthorized access to computer systems.
  • Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information.
  • Cyberbullying: Harassment or bullying using digital platforms.

8.4 Malicious Software and Spam

Malicious software (malware) includes viruses, worms, trojans, and ransomware designed to harm or exploit systems. Spam refers to unsolicited messages, often sent in bulk, typically for advertising or phishing purposes.

Examples of Malware

  • Viruses: Programs that replicate and spread to other systems.
  • Ransomware: Locks users out of their systems until a ransom is paid.
  • Spyware: Secretly monitors user activity.

8.5 Protection from Cybercrime

Protecting yourself from cybercrime involves using strong passwords, keeping software updated, and being cautious online. Tools like antivirus software and firewalls can also help.

Best Practices

  • Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading unknown files.

8.6 Intellectual Property Rights

Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) protect creations of the mind, such as inventions, literary works, and designs. They ensure creators receive recognition and financial benefits for their work.

Types of IPR

  • Copyright: Protects literary and artistic works.
  • Patents: Protect inventions and innovations.
  • Trademarks: Protect brand names and logos.

8.7 Concept of Digital Signature

A digital signature is a cryptographic tool used to verify the authenticity and integrity of digital documents. It ensures that a document has not been altered and confirms the identity of the sender.

How It Works

  • A unique digital fingerprint is created for the document.
  • The fingerprint is encrypted using the sender's private key.
  • The recipient decrypts it using the sender's public key to verify authenticity.

8.8 Concept of Cyber Law in Nepal

Cyber law in Nepal refers to the legal framework governing the use of the internet and digital technologies. It addresses issues like cybercrime, data protection, and electronic transactions.

Key Provisions

  • Electronic Transactions Act: Regulates online transactions and digital signatures.
  • Cybercrime Laws: Address hacking, data theft, and other cyber offenses.

8.9 ICT Policy in Nepal

The ICT Policy in Nepal aims to promote the development and use of information and communication technologies to drive economic growth and improve public services.

Objectives

  • Expand internet access to rural areas.
  • Promote digital literacy and skills development.
  • Encourage innovation and entrepreneurship in the tech sector.
